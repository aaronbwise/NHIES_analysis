The World Bank and FAO developed the ADePT-Food Security Module (ADePT-FSM) in 2012, under the auspices of the European Union Improved Global Governance for Hunger Reduction Program.

ADePT-FSM is a standalone, free software that allows users to derive consistent food security statistics at national and subnational levels from food acquisition and consumption data collected in national household surveys (e.g., HIES). The software depends on three levels of data from the HIES: (1) the household dataset, (2) the household members dataset, and (3) the household income and expenditures in goods and services dataset, including food. ADePT-FSM also requires a fourth dataset including exogenous data on the nutrient content (proteins, carbohydrates, etc.) of the food commodities listed in the survey. Such data are found in food composition tables (FCTs) available for many countries all over the world.

These statistics are presented in the form of readily available Excel tables and include: the average consumption of dietary energy, macronutrients, micronutrients and amino acids; the distribution of dietary energy consumption, and the estimated proportion of people that is likely to be undernourished in a population.
